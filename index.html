Who is George Orwell?

George Orwell (1903-1950)

• Real name: Eric Arthur Blair
• Born in British India, educated in England at Eton College
• Served as a police officer in Burma (Myanmar), which shaped his anti-imperialist views
• Fought in the Spanish Civil War (1936-1939) against fascism
• Witnessed firsthand the dangers of totalitarianism and propaganda
• Known for clear, direct prose and fierce opposition to totalitarianism
• Also wrote "1984," another famous dystopian novel
• Died of tuberculosis at age 46, shortly after publishing "1984"

Historical Context
What events inspired Animal Farm? →
The Russian Revolution & Stalin Era

• Written as an allegory of the Russian Revolution (1917) and Stalin's rise to power
• Published in 1945, near the end of World War II
• Critiques how the idealistic revolution was corrupted by power
• The Bolshevik Revolution promised equality but resulted in dictatorship
• Stalin's totalitarian regime (1924-1953) used propaganda, fear, and violence
• The novel was rejected by several publishers for being too critical of the Soviet Union
• Serves as a warning about how revolutions can be betrayed by their leaders

Setting & Structure
Where and when does it take place? →
Manor Farm (later Animal Farm)

• Rural farm in England
• Represents Russia and the Soviet Union
• Time period is deliberately vague but parallels 1917-1940s
• The farm becomes increasingly isolated from the outside world
• Simple setting allows focus on characters and political commentary

Story Structure:
The novella is divided into 10 chapters, following the arc from revolution through corruption:
1. The dream of rebellion (Old Major's speech)
2. The rebellion itself
3. Establishment of new order
4. Power struggles
5. Consolidation of dictatorship
6. Complete corruption and betrayal
🎭
Major Themes
What are the central themes? →
  • Corruption of Power: How absolute power corrupts absolutely, and revolutionary ideals are betrayed by those in power
  • Class Struggle: The conflict between workers and ruling class, and how new hierarchies replace old ones
  • Propaganda & Manipulation: How language is used to control thought and justify oppression
  • The Dangers of Ignorance: How lack of education makes the masses vulnerable to manipulation
  • Totalitarianism: The methods dictators use to maintain control through fear, propaganda, and violence
  • Betrayal of Ideals: How revolutionary principles are gradually abandoned for personal gain
  • False Equality: The irony of "All animals are equal, but some animals are more equal than others"

Literary Concepts
What makes this story unique? →
Allegory
Animal Farm is a political allegory where characters and events represent real historical figures and events from the Russian Revolution. Each animal symbolizes a type of person or political figure.

Satire
Orwell uses humor, irony, and exaggeration to criticize totalitarianism and expose the hypocrisy of corrupt leaders. The absurdity of pigs walking on two legs highlights the ridiculousness of their betrayal.

Fable Structure
Like traditional fables, the story uses animals to teach a moral lesson. However, instead of a simple moral, Orwell presents complex political commentary.

Symbolism
• The Farm: Russia/Soviet Union
• The Windmill: Stalin's Five-Year Plans
• The Seven Commandments: Communist ideology that gets corrupted
• The Song "Beasts of England": Revolutionary anthem and hope
